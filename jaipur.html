Jaipur's Must-See Gems

Hawa Mahal

A stunning palace with intricate honeycomb-like façade, offering panoramic views of Jaipur.

Amber Fort

A majestic hilltop fortress showcasing a blend of Hindu and Mughal architecture.

Jal Mahal

A majestic hilltop fortress showcasing a blend of Hindu and Mughal architecture.

Jantar Mantar

A UNESCO World Heritage site, this observatory houses various architectural and astrological instruments.

Jaipur Zoo

Located near the Albert Hall Museum, the zoo is home to a variety of animals and is a great place for a family outing.

Famous festivals

Jaipur Literature Festival

Held annually in January, the Jaipur Literature Festival is one of the largest literary festivals in the world.

Gangaur Festival

Celebrated in March or April, Gangaur is one of the most important festivals for women in Rajasthan, honoring the goddess Gauri .

Teej

Teej is another significant festival for women, celebrated in July or August dedicated to goddess Parvati.

Makar Sankranti

Celebrated in January, Makar Sankranti marks the transition of the sun into the zodiac sign of Capricorn.

Taj Mahotsav

Taj Mahotsav held in February is a cultural extravaganza featuring music, dance, crafts, and culinary delights.

Famous Markets

Johari Bazar

Johari Bazaar: Located in the heart of the Pink City, Johari Bazaar is renowned for its exquisite collection of jewelry, including precious gemstones, traditional Kundan, Meenakari, and Thewa jewelry. It's also a great place to shop for textiles, sarees, lehengas, and other traditional Rajasthani attire.

Bapu Bazaar

Bapu Bazaar: This bustling market is famous for its colorful Rajasthani fabrics, including Bandhani and Leheriya sarees, as well as block-printed textiles. Visitors can also find a wide variety of handicrafts, including pottery, leather goods, and embroidered fabrics.

Nehru Bazaar

Nehru Bazaar is a popular market in Jaipur, known for its diverse range of products, including textiles, handicrafts, perfumes, and footwear. It's particularly famous for its collection of mojris (traditional leather footwear) and juttis (embroidered shoes), as well as its delicious street food stalls offering local delicacies.

Chandpole Bazaar

Chandpole Bazaar is another bustling market in Jaipur, famous for its traditional Rajasthani textiles, including bandhani sarees, leheriya dupattas, and block-printed fabrics. It's also known for its wide range of handicrafts, including blue pottery, miniature paintings, and marble carvings, making it a favorite destination for art and craft lovers.

Tripolia Bazaar, Jaipur

Sadar Bazaar, Jaisalmer: Located near the Jaisalmer Fort, Sadar Bazaar is famous for its Rajasthani handicrafts, including embroidered fabrics, leather goods, camel leather products, and traditional Rajasthani puppets. The market also offers a variety of colorful textiles, rugs, and carpets.

Medical Facilities

Sawai Man Singh Hospital (SMS Hospital)

This is one of the largest government hospitals in Jaipur and provides comprehensive healthcare services across various specialties.

Location: Jawahar Lal Nehru Marg, Jaipur, Rajasthan

Helpline: +91 141 2560291

Fortis Escorts Hospital

A prominent private hospital in the city known for its advanced medical facilities and expertise in cardiology, orthopedics, oncology, and other specialties.

Location: Jawahar Lal Nehru Marg, Malviya Nagar, Jaipur, Rajasthan

Helpline: +91 141 2547000

Manipal Hospitals

Another well-known private hospital chain with a presence in Jaipur, offering multi-specialty healthcare services including cardiology, neurology, orthopedics, and more.

Location: Sector 5, Main Sikar Road, Vidhyadhar Nagar, Jaipur, Rajasthan

Helpline: +91 141 5166000

Eternal Heart Care Centre (EHCC)

Specializing in cardiac care, EHCC is a leading hospital in Jaipur equipped with state-of-the-art facilities for cardiac surgeries, interventions, and treatments.

Location: 92, Kumbha Marg, Sector 11, Pratap Nagar, Jaipur, Rajasthan

Helpline: +91 141 5164444
